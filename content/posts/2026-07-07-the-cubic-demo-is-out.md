## Hello!

Welcome to the Cubic devlog.

This game has been in development for a long time, but we are finally approaching the finish line, and ready to show it off to the public.

Cubic is being developed by three people: Victor (Project Lead, Game Designer, Sound & Music), Axel (Artist, Worldbuilder), and Ahmad (Programmer). We started development during university, but have been taking it seriously as a project since day 1. We have learned much about game development over the years and the game has only gotten better.

The Steam Page provides a description of the game, but at its core, from a developers perspective, Cubic is a game about exploring - exploring a world and exploring a ruleset. We recommend you go into it blind, so I won't be going into too much detail in these devlogs, but there is much to uncover. Cubic also tries very hard to be a minimalistic game that is easy to get into and fun to play. Every puzzle showcases a new idea without adding much new complexity. Every world screen is handcrafted and contains new gameplay or tells something about the world.

If the game sounds or looks interesting, you can now play the demo! It contains the tutorial areas of the game and takes about 30-60 minutes to beat. We decided on this scope to keep the experience light and playable in one sitting. One thing we sacrifice by doing this is not really showing the open-world nature of the game. In the full game you are more open to explore in the directions that appeal to you.

## Demo, Trailer, and Viborg Game Expo

[Watch the demo trailer here!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E4wOiwcpgao)

We launched the Cubic Demo, along with a trailer, on the 26th of June during our attendance of the Danish gaming event Viborg Game Expo. The demo has been ready for some time now, but a never-ending list of polish and minor fixes has kept us from releasing it sooner.

We had a great time showcasing the demo (and more!) at the Expo. Thank you to everyone who played Cubic there, we met some great people and went home inspired to work even harder.

Below is a FAQ about the Demo and that is the end of this first devlog. Be sure to keep up to date on our social medias and feel free to contact us there! Thank you for checking out Cubic.

-Victor

## FAQ about the Demo

* *What platforms is the demo available on?*

For now, only Windows. The shader-graphics are currently causing problems on Linux and Mac. We will make an update as soon as it is playable on other platforms.

* *Is controller/gamepad supported?*

Gamepad is supported but the UI prompts show the keyboard bindings only.

* *What languages are supported?*

Text is only used for menus and tooltips. The languages we support for this purpose are

* English
* Danish
* German
* Spanish
* French
* Portuguese (BR)
* Romanian
* Finnish
* Malay
* Russian
* Japanese
* Chinese (Traditional)
* Chinese (Simplified)
* Korean
* Arabic

The original text was written in English. If you find any translation unsatisfactory, feel free to contact us! (On Discord we have a channel dedicated to translation comments)

* *How long is the demo?*

It takes most players around 30-45 minutes to complete the demo.

* *How long is the full game?*

The full game will be substantially larger. The demo is somewhat a tutorial for the full game, so expect a much bigger world to explore - one that constantly presents new ideas and puzzles.

It is of course still very much in development, so a precise number of play hours is hard to give.
