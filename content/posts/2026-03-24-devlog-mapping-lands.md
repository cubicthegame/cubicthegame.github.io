Every land in Cubic starts as a single rule scrawled on a whiteboard. Then we ask the only question that matters: what's the most surprising thing this rule can do once the player has seen it three times?

## Grey-boxing

We block out a land first with flat grey tiles and no art at all, so the puzzle has to work on its own terms before it gets to lean on atmosphere.

## Then the mood arrives

Once the layout holds up, art and lighting move in — the color grading, the ambient audio, the small environmental details that make a land feel lived-in and recently abandoned.
